id: 04-colposcopy
title: What is a colposcopy?
origin: ACOG FAQ 135 — Colposcopy
---

A **colposcopy** is a way for the doctor to take a closer look at the cervix. It is done in the clinic. It usually takes about **15 to 20 minutes**.

What happens during a colposcopy:

1. You lie back on the exam table, similar to a Pap test.
2. The doctor uses a tool called a **colposcope**. It is like a small lighted microscope on a stand. **The colposcope stays outside your body.** It does not go inside you.
3. The doctor places a speculum (the same tool used for a Pap test) so the cervix can be seen.
4. A **vinegar solution** is gently applied to the cervix. It may cause a brief stinging or cold feeling. The vinegar makes any abnormal areas turn white so the doctor can see them.
5. If the doctor sees an area that looks different, a very small piece of tissue (a **biopsy**) may be taken to send to the lab. A biopsy feels like a quick pinch or a strong cramp, then it is over.

After the colposcopy:

- You can drive yourself home.
- You may have light spotting or a dark discharge (from the medicine put on after a biopsy) for a few days. This is normal.
- Mild cramping is common. Over-the-counter pain medicine like ibuprofen usually helps.
- If a biopsy was taken, avoid tampons, douching, and vaginal sex for about a week, or as your doctor tells you.
- Biopsy results usually come back in **1 to 2 weeks**.

A colposcopy is not surgery. You are awake. You do not need anesthesia. You do not need to stop work for more than the day of the visit.
