This is the cervical cancer fact sheet from the Kentucky Cancer Program at the University of Kentucky.

**What is cervical cancer?**
Cervical cancer is when abnormal cells begin to develop and, if not removed, can become cancer.

**Symptoms.**
Early stages of cervical cancer usually have no symptoms. When the cancer is more advanced, the most common symptom is abnormal bleeding from the vagina.

**Screening.**
Healthcare providers screen using a Pap test (also called a Pap smear) and an HPV test. These tests only take a few seconds and are usually painless. **Cervical cancer is almost 100 percent curable when found early.**

**When to be screened.**
- Age 21: Start having Pap tests every 3 years.
- Age 30: Pap test every 3 years and HPV test every 5 years, or both together every 5 years.
- Age 65+: Talk with your doctor about when to stop.

**How to help prevent cervical cancer:**
- Get regular Pap tests as recommended.
- Vaccinate against HPV between ages 9 and 26.
- Do not smoke. Smoking nearly doubles your chance of cervical cancer.
- Limit number of sexual partners and use condoms.

**HPV vaccination schedule (Markey/KCP):**
- Before age 15: 2 doses.
- Age 15 and older: 3 doses.
- Ages 26+: talk with your doctor.

**If you cannot afford the tests.**
Most cancer screenings are covered by insurance, Medicare, and Medicaid. Most plans also cover HPV vaccinations for ages 11–26. If you do not have insurance, the **Kentucky Women's Cancer Screening Program** may help. **Call 844-249-0708.**
