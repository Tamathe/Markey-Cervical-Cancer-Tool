id: 09-cost-and-coverage
title: Cost, insurance, and help in Kentucky
origin: Kentucky Cancer Program / Markey Cancer Center
---

**Most cervical cancer screenings are covered by insurance, Medicare, and Medicaid.** This includes Pap tests, HPV tests, and follow-up colposcopy when it is medically needed.

**Most insurance plans cover HPV vaccination** for ages 11 to 26 at no cost to you.

**If you do not have insurance, the Kentucky Women's Cancer Screening Program (KWCSP) may help.** This program offers free or low-cost Pap tests, HPV tests, follow-up colposcopy, and related care to women in Kentucky who qualify based on income.

To find out if you qualify: **call 844-249-0708**.

**At Markey Cancer Center**, the financial counseling team can give you an estimate of what your visit might cost before you come in. If you are worried about cost, do not skip the appointment — call and ask first. There is almost always a way.

**The Kentucky Cancer Program (KCP) Pathfinder** can also connect you with local support services in your area: www.kycancerprogram.org.

If transportation, childcare, or time off work are the barrier — not the bill — a **patient navigator** can help with those too. Ask the clinic for a navigator, or ask the bot to send a message on your behalf.
