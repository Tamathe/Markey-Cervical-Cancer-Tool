title: HPV, Pap results, and pregnancy
origin: ACOG — Cervical Cancer Screening in Pregnancy
---

If you are pregnant, an abnormal Pap or a positive HPV result is rarely an emergency, and the plan is usually different than it would be otherwise.

**Pap and HPV testing can be done during pregnancy.** They are safe for the pregnancy and for the baby.

**Colposcopy is safe during pregnancy.** The colposcope stays outside the body. Most doctors will not take a biopsy of certain inner tissues during pregnancy, but they can still take a careful look.

**Treatment is usually delayed until after delivery.** For most pre-cancer changes, doctors will watch closely during the pregnancy and re-evaluate after the baby is born — about 6 weeks postpartum. Most pre-cancer changes do not get worse during pregnancy, and some even improve.

**HPV is not passed to the baby in most cases.** The very rare risk of passing HPV during a vaginal birth does not change how doctors recommend delivery — vaginal birth is still safe in almost all cases.

**Cervical cancer during pregnancy is rare** (about 1 to 3 cases per 10,000 pregnancies). When it does happen, a team of specialists works with you to plan care that protects you and the pregnancy.

If you are pregnant and you have an abnormal Pap or HPV result, tell your obstetrician. They will work with the rest of your care team on the right timing for follow-up.
