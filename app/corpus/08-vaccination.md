The HPV vaccine protects against the strains of HPV most likely to cause cancer. The current vaccine in the United States (Gardasil 9) covers **9 types** of HPV.

**Who should get it:**
- Routine vaccination is recommended for all boys and girls starting at **age 9**, ideally before any sexual activity.
- Catch-up vaccination is recommended through **age 26** if not already vaccinated.
- Ages 27 to 45: shared decision with your doctor. The vaccine is approved in this age range, but the benefit is smaller because many adults have already been exposed to some HPV types.

**How many doses:**
- If you start **before your 15th birthday**: **2 doses**, 6 to 12 months apart.
- If you start **at age 15 or later**: **3 doses** over 6 months.

**Cost:** Most insurance plans, Medicare, and Medicaid cover HPV vaccination for ages 11 to 26. The federal **Vaccines for Children** program covers children up to age 18 who are uninsured or underinsured.

**Does the vaccine work if I already have HPV?** It does not clear an infection you already have. But because there are many HPV types, it can still protect you against the ones you have not yet been exposed to.

**Is it safe?** The HPV vaccine has been used since 2006 in the United States. Hundreds of millions of doses have been given worldwide. The main side effects are soreness at the injection site, a brief fever, or feeling tired for a day or two. Serious side effects are very rare.

**Does it replace Pap testing?** No. People who are vaccinated still need regular Pap and HPV screening, because the vaccine does not cover every type of HPV.
