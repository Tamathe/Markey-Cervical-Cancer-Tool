title: What is HPV?
origin: CDC — Human Papillomavirus (HPV)

---

HPV stands for **human papillomavirus**. It is the most common sexually transmitted infection in the United States. There are more than 200 types of HPV. About 14 of them are called "high-risk" because they can cause cancer if they stay in the body for many years.

HPV is spread through close skin-to-skin contact during sex. This includes vaginal, anal, and oral sex. A person can have HPV and never know it, because HPV usually has no symptoms.

About **8 out of 10 sexually active people** get HPV at some point in their life. For most people, the body's immune system clears the virus on its own within 1 to 2 years. A small number of HPV infections do not clear and can slowly cause changes in cells. These cell changes are what doctors watch for — because if they are found early, they can be treated before they ever become cancer.

HPV can cause six different cancers: cervical, anal, oropharyngeal (throat), vulvar, vaginal, and penile. Cervical cancer is the most common.

HPV is not the same as herpes or HIV. It is a separate virus.
