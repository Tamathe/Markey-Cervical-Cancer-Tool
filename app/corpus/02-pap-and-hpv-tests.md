A **Pap test** (also called a Pap smear) and an **HPV test** are two different tests, but they are usually done at the same visit, with the same swab.

During the test, a health care provider uses a small brush to collect cells from the cervix. The cervix is the lower part of the uterus, at the top of the vagina. The test takes only a few seconds. Most people feel light pressure, not pain.

The **Pap test** looks at the cells under a microscope. It is looking for changes in the cells that could be early signs of cancer — changes that can be treated long before they become cancer.

The **HPV test** looks at the same cells for the high-risk types of HPV, the virus that causes most cervical cancers. The HPV test does not tell you when you got the virus or who you got it from.

**When to be screened.** Two sets of expert guidelines are used in the U.S. They mostly agree. Your doctor will follow one of them.

- **U.S. Preventive Services Task Force (the most common):**
  - Age 21–29: Pap test every 3 years.
  - Age 30–65: Pap plus HPV test every 5 years, **or** HPV test alone every 5 years, **or** Pap alone every 3 years.
  - Over 65: You can usually stop, if your past tests have been normal.

- **American Cancer Society (updated in 2020):**
  - Age 25–65: HPV test alone every 5 years (preferred). Co-testing every 5 years or Pap alone every 3 years is also fine.
  - Over 65: You can usually stop, if your past tests have been normal.

**About results timing.** Results usually come back from the lab in **1 to 3 weeks**.

**You may need to be screened more often** if you have HIV, a weakened immune system, a recent abnormal result, a history of cervical cancer, or if your mother took the drug DES while pregnant with you.

**You may not need to be screened at all** if you had your whole uterus and cervix removed for a reason that was not cancer. If only the uterus was removed and the cervix is still there, keep getting screened.

If your test is abnormal, it does not mean you have cancer. It means a closer look or a follow-up test is needed.
